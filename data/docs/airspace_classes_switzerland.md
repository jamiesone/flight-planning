# Airspace Classes in Switzerland 

**Sources:** ICAO Annex 11, SERA Appendix 4 (EU 2016/1185), Swiss AIP ENR 1.4, BAZL Directive on Application of Airspace Classes (VRV-L Annex 1)  

---

## Class C

**Type:** Controlled airspace  
**Where used in Switzerland:** Above FL195 (above ~5,900m AMSL); also surrounds major international airports (Zurich, Geneva) from a defined lower limit upward as Terminal Maneuvering Areas (TMAs)  
**Lower limit:** Varies by sector — typically FL100 or higher in TMA structures  
**Upper limit:** FL660

**Rules for VFR (balloon):**
- ATC clearance required before entry
- Continuous two-way radio communication required
- Separation from IFR traffic provided by ATC
- Traffic information on other VFR traffic provided
- Speed limit: 250 kt IAS below FL100 (not relevant for balloons)

**Practical implication for balloons:** Class C airspace begins well above typical balloon cruise altitudes. A balloon operating below FL100 (~3,050m AMSL) is unlikely to enter Class C unless flying in a TMA sector with a low base. Always check the vertical limits of the TMA for your departure region.

---

## Class D

**Type:** Controlled airspace  
**Where used in Switzerland:** Control Zones (CTRs) around regional airports (e.g. Bern-Belp, Lugano, Sion, Buochs, Emmen); some TMA sectors  
**Typical vertical extent:** Surface (SFC) to a defined upper limit, usually between 1,500m and 3,000m AMSL depending on the airport

**Rules for VFR (balloon):**
- ATC clearance required before entry
- Continuous two-way radio communication required
- No ATC separation from other VFR traffic — traffic information only
- Pilot responsible for separation from other VFR aircraft

**Practical implication for balloons:** Class D CTRs are the most common controlled airspace a balloon will encounter at low altitude. You must obtain ATC clearance before entering. Without a radio, entry is not permitted. Many balloon launch sites near populated valleys may be adjacent to or within Class D CTRs — this must be checked for every flight.

**Required action:** Contact the relevant ATC unit (frequency published in AIP AD section for each aerodrome) and request clearance. If denied or unable to contact, do not enter.

## Class E

**Type:** Controlled airspace (IFR only for separation purposes)  
**Where used in Switzerland:** Between 2,000ft AGL (~600m AGL) and FL130 in the Alps; between 2,000ft AGL and FL100 on the Swiss Plateau/Jura — forms the bulk of mid-altitude airspace above the Class G base  
**Lower limit:** Typically 2,000ft AGL (approximately 600m above ground)  
**Upper limit:** FL100 (Swiss Plateau/Jura) or FL130 (Alps, during MIL ON hours)

**Rules for VFR (balloon):**
- No ATC clearance required
- No radio communication required (unless in an RMZ or TMZ overlay)
- No separation from IFR traffic provided — pilot must see and avoid
- Traffic information provided where possible

**Practical implication for balloons:** Class E is where most balloon cruise flight takes place in Switzerland. No clearance is needed, but IFR traffic (which may be operating in cloud) shares this airspace. Balloons must maintain VFR cloud clearance requirements: **1,500m horizontal and 300m (1,000ft) vertical distance from cloud** and minimum flight visibility of **5km** (below FL100).

Note: During MIL ON hours (Monday–Friday 0730–1205 LT and 1315–1705 LT), the upper limit of Class E in the Alps is FL130 (~3,950m AMSL). Outside these hours it drops.

## Class G

**Type:** Uncontrolled airspace  
**Where used in Switzerland:** From the surface up to 2,000ft AGL (~600m AGL) everywhere except within CTRs; effectively the low-altitude layer across Switzerland  
**Upper limit:** 2,000ft AGL (where Class E begins above)

**Rules for VFR (balloon):**
- No ATC clearance required
- No radio communication required (unless in an RMZ overlay)
- No ATC separation or traffic information
- Pilot fully responsible for see-and-avoid

**Practical implication for balloons:** Takeoff and initial climb, and final descent to landing, occur in Class G. Minimum VFR conditions in Class G at low altitude (below 3,500ft AMSL or 1,000ft AGL, whichever is higher): **clear of cloud, ground in sight**, and minimum visibility **1,500m** (reduced from 5km, applicable at speeds ≤140kt IAS — balloons always qualify).

**Transponder in Class G:** There is no blanket transponder requirement tied to Class G or to a height AGL. A Mode S transponder must be carried and operated only inside a Transponder Mandatory Zone (TMZ) (BAZL LR I-003). Many balloons are not transponder-equipped and so must remain clear of any TMZ.

## VFR Ceiling & Onboard Equipment

**Sources:** VRV-L Appendix 139 (FL195 ceiling); SERA / NCO operational rules (oxygen).

- **VFR ceiling:** VFR flight is not permitted above **FL195** (~5,950m AMSL) — the top of VFR airspace in Switzerland; Class C above FL195 is IFR only. A balloon must remain below FL195 at all times.
- **Supplemental oxygen:** oxygen must be carried and used on board above **3,000m AMSL**. A balloon climbing into the higher wind layers (e.g. 700/600 hPa) to change drift direction must have oxygen available.
- **Transponder:** there is no height-based transponder mandate; a Mode S transponder is required only inside a **TMZ** (see the Class G note above).
